What is XRP?

XRP is the native currency of the Ripple network, which enables instant and nearly free payments in any currency based on the Ripple protocol. Ripple Labs (the team behind the Ripple network) created 100 billion XRP at Ripple’s inception. Because Ripple is a decentralized payment network and distributed currency exchange, Ripple Labs plans to freely give away at least 55 percent of the total XRP. Learn more at Ripple.com

What is the WCG?

The WCG (World Community Grid) is an effort to create the world’s largest public computing grid to tackle scientific research projects. Using the idle time of computers around the world, World Community Grid’s research projects have analyzed aspects of the human genome, HIV, dengue, muscular dystrophy, cancer, influenza, rice crop yields, and clean energy. Learn more at Worldcommunitygrid.org

How does donating work?

When your device is on but not in use, it will donate its computing power to scientific projects of your choice. Your device will request data on a specific project from the WCG server. It will then perform computations on this data, send the results back to the server, and ask the server for a new piece of work. Each computation that your device performs provides scientists with critical information that accelerates the pace of research!

How many XRP will I get?

First, you’ll sign up for the WCG and create a Ripple account at www.ripple.com/client. Once you begin computing for good, you’ll generate “WCG points,” which then get redeemed as XRP in your Ripple account. Each day, we’ll post the day’s XRP to WCG point ratio, which will be adjusted depending on how much total computing power is being donated.

When will I get XRP?

The WCG awards points at 0:00 Coordinated Universal Time (UTC) each day. After Ripple Labs receives point awards, we then issue XRP based on the posted exchange rate between WCG points and XRP. Then, we calculate and post a new daily exchange rate.

What can I do with XRP?

Some people like to keep XRP. You can send it to others with a Ripple account, or trade it into different currencies. We recommend checking out the International Ripple Business Association or Ripple Federation for a list of developers and businesses who use the Ripple network.
